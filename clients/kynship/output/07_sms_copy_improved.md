| Sounds human & casual? | Mostly yes, but "new customer revenue" is corporate | "fix it upstream by engineering the CAC ceiling off your P&L" is nerdy/jargon-heavy | "engineered before it" reads like a whitepaper, not a text |

**Verdict:** All three variants fail the character limit by 40-70%. The proof points and mechanisms are strong but need radical compression. The jargon ("CAC ceiling off your P&L," "engineering," "seeding-driven assets") needs to be translated into how a founder actually talks over drinks.

## Improved SMS Variants

### SMS-01 — Pain + Proof via Mechanism (Problem-Aware)

**Text 1:**
> Hi {{first_name}}, {{sender_name}} from Kynship. Random one but we took Sweet Bee Organics from 300k to 1.5m in new revenue in a single month on Meta.

**Text 2:**
> Built their profit ceiling first, then flooded 250+ fresh creatives a month through seeding. Worth a look for {{company_name}}?

- **Text 1:** 156 characters
- **Text 2:** 131 characters
- **Combined:** 287 characters

**What changed:**
- "new customer revenue" → "new revenue" (how founders actually say it)
- Killed the mechanism over-explanation in Text 2. "Built their profit ceiling first" replaces the P&L jargon — same idea, bar conversation language
- "200 to 300 creative assets a month from influencer seeding" → "250+ fresh creatives a month through seeding" — one number, not a range, tighter
- CTA shortened from "Wondering if it's worth a quick look for {{company_name}} sometime next week?" to "Worth a look for {{company_name}}?" — less needy, more casual

---

### SMS-02 — Current Way Failing + Mechanism + Proof (Solution-Aware)

**Text 1:**
> Hey {{first_name}}, {{sender_name}} from Kynship. Spending 6 to 9k a month on UGC and still watching ads die in 3 to 5 days?

**Text 2:**
> Gardencup had the same problem. We set profit guardrails first, ran 250+ seeding creatives a month. 260k to 776k new revenue in month one. Worth 15 min?

- **Text 1:** 126 characters
- **Text 2:** 153 characters
- **Combined:** 279 characters

**What changed:**
- "Most DTC brands we talk to are spending" → "Spending" — kills the mass-blast tell, makes it direct
- "CAC won't come back down" dropped — the UGC fatigue pain is stronger and more specific for solution-aware prospects
- "fix it upstream by engineering the CAC ceiling off your P&L" → "set profit guardrails first" — same concept, human words
- "Open to a 15 min look for {{company_name}}?" → "Worth 15 min?" — tighter, less formal

---

### SMS-03 — Pain + Reframe: Achieve Through X Not Y (Most-Aware)

**Text 1:**
> Hi {{first_name}}, {{sender_name}} from Kynship. If Meta CAC keeps creeping and UGC is on repeat, the ceiling is being chased not set.

**Text 2:**
> Supergut set it from the P&L first. 25k to 500k a month in ad spend in 3 months, profitably. Worth a quick chat for {{company_name}}?

- **Text 1:** 137 characters
- **Text 2:** 134 characters
- **Combined:** 271 characters

**What changed:**
- "Quick one" dropped — nice but costs 10 chars
- "200 to 300 seeding-driven assets a month instead of the usual UGC retainer loop" — completely cut. Most-aware prospects don't need the mechanism explained, they need the reframe. The reframe ("chased not set") does the work
- "Worth 15 mins to see if the same fits {{company_name}}?" → "Worth a quick chat for {{company_name}}?" — more casual

## Recommended Pick

**SMS-01 (improved) is the strongest cold opener.** The proof is the most lopsided (300k to 1.5m in one month), it fits under 300 characters, and it reads like an actual text message. The mechanism is compressed to one line instead of a paragraph.

**A/B test:** SMS-01 vs SMS-02. SMS-01 leads with proof for problem-aware prospects. SMS-02 leads with the UGC pain pattern for solution-aware prospects who've already burned money on creative and will immediately recognize themselves in "ads die in 3 to 5 days."

SMS-03 is the specialist variant — reserve for prospects you know have churned an agency or two and are evaluating alternatives. The "chased not set" reframe only lands if they've already lived the chase.
