# The Sandler Rules — Quick Reference (49 Rules)

Source: *The Sandler Rules* by David Sandler / Dave Mattson. One-line summaries of what each rule means and how to apply it in sales/GTM work.

---

## Part One — Core Concepts (Rules 1–6)

**1. You have to learn to fail, to win.**
- *What it does:* Reframes rejection as data, not defeat.
- *How to use:* Track "no"s as progress markers; debrief each loss for one lesson.

**2. Don't spill your candy in the lobby.**
- *What it does:* Stops you from dumping features/pitch before earning the right.
- *How to use:* Hold back product info until the prospect reveals pain, budget, and decision process.

**3. No mutual mystification.**
- *What it does:* Forces explicit agreements instead of vague mutual assumptions.
- *How to use:* End every meeting with a written next-step contract: who does what, by when.

**4. A decision not to make a decision is a decision.**
- *What it does:* Refuses to accept "I need to think about it" as a neutral outcome.
- *How to use:* Treat stalls as a "no" and push for a real yes/no rather than letting deals drift.

**5. Never answer an unasked question.**
- *What it does:* Prevents volunteering objections the prospect didn't raise.
- *How to use:* Answer only what's asked; don't pre-empt concerns — you'll plant them.

**6. Don't buy back tomorrow the product or service you sold today.**
- *What it does:* Blocks post-sale doubt that leads to refunds/churn.
- *How to use:* Lock the decision with the prospect at close; don't "reassure" after — it invites second-guessing.

## Part Two — Execute (Rules 7–33)

**7. You never have to like prospecting, you just have to do it.**
- *What it does:* Removes emotion from a required behavior.
- *How to use:* Make prospecting a non-negotiable calendar block; feelings don't enter the decision.

**8. When prospecting, go for the appointment.**
- *What it does:* Keeps the call's objective narrow.
- *How to use:* On cold outreach, sell only the meeting — not the product.

**9. Every unsuccessful prospecting call earns compound interest.**
- *What it does:* Statistical mindset — each "no" increases odds of the next "yes."
- *How to use:* Know your conversion ratios; celebrate quota of attempts, not just wins.

**10. Develop a prospecting awareness.**
- *What it does:* Turns every interaction into a potential pipeline source.
- *How to use:* Always be listening for referral triggers, adjacent problems, intros.

**11. Money does grow on trees.**
- *What it does:* Referrals = tree branches; each client grows more.
- *How to use:* Ask every closed client for 2–3 intros before leaving the room.

**12. Answer every question with a question.**
- *What it does:* Keeps you in control and uncovers real intent.
- *How to use:* "Why do you ask?" / "What's behind that?" before answering anything.

**13. No mind reading.**
- *What it does:* Blocks assumptions about what the prospect means or feels.
- *How to use:* Ask clarifying questions; never interpret silence or vague words on your own.

**14. A prospect who is listening is no prospect at all.**
- *What it does:* Flips the presentation model — they should talk, not you.
- *How to use:* If you're monologuing, you're losing. Make them do 70%+ of the talking.

**15. The best sales presentation you'll ever give, the prospect will never see.**
- *What it does:* The deal is won in discovery, not in the deck.
- *How to use:* Only present after you've qualified pain, budget, decision — the "pitch" is a formality.

**16. Never ask for the order — make the prospect give up.**
- *What it does:* Pressure-free close; let them ask to buy.
- *How to use:* Qualify so hard that they sell themselves and request the next step.

**17. The professional does what he did as a dummy on purpose.**
- *What it does:* Intentional use of "beginner" behaviors (playing naive, humble).
- *How to use:* Drop the polished act — "I may be missing something, help me understand…" disarms prospects.

**18. Don't paint "seagulls" in your prospect's picture.**
- *What it does:* Don't add features/promises they didn't ask for.
- *How to use:* Solve only the pain they stated; extras inflate expectations and kill deals.

**19. Never help the prospect end the interview.**
- *What it does:* Stops premature wrap-ups.
- *How to use:* When they say "well, this sounds great…" — don't nod along; probe deeper.

**20. The bottom line of professional selling is going to the bank.**
- *What it does:* Activity ≠ progress. Closed revenue is the only scoreboard.
- *How to use:* Measure deals, not meetings. Cut activity that doesn't lead to close.

**21. Sell today, educate tomorrow.**
- *What it does:* Don't waste the sales call teaching; they buy first, learn later.
- *How to use:* Reserve product training for post-purchase; don't give free consulting.

**22. Only give a presentation for the "kill."**
- *What it does:* Presentation = final step, not discovery tool.
- *How to use:* Never demo without pre-agreed decision criteria and a clear yes/no outcome.

**23. The way to get rid of a bomb is to defuse it before it blows up.**
- *What it does:* Surface objections early, not at close.
- *How to use:* Proactively raise risks/concerns during discovery so they're resolved before pricing.

**24. Product knowledge used at the wrong time can be intimidating.**
- *What it does:* Over-explaining scares buyers and kills trust.
- *How to use:* Match technical depth to what they asked; don't show off.

**25. When you want to know the future, bring it back to the present.**
- *What it does:* Make them commit now to what they'll do later.
- *How to use:* "If I showed you X today, what would you do?" — force present-tense decisions.

**26. People buy in spite of the hard sell, not because of it.**
- *What it does:* Pressure costs deals.
- *How to use:* Posture of take-it-or-leave-it; walk away more often than you push.

**27. You can't sell anybody anything — they must discover they want it.**
- *What it does:* Buying is self-persuasion, not convincing.
- *How to use:* Ask questions that let them articulate their own need, in their own words.

**28. When under attack, fall back.**
- *What it does:* Defuse hostility by retreating, not defending.
- *How to use:* "You may be right" / "Let's not worry about that" — don't argue; reduce temperature.

**29. Your meter's always running.**
- *What it does:* Your time has a cost; act like it.
- *How to use:* Qualify hard and disqualify fast; don't give free hours to tire-kickers.

**30. You can't lose anything you don't have.**
- *What it does:* Removes fear of rejection on unsigned deals.
- *How to use:* A deal isn't yours until it's closed — act boldly because there's nothing to protect.

**31. Close the sale or close the file.**
- *What it does:* No perpetual follow-ups.
- *How to use:* Force a decision. If no, file it and move on — don't keep "nurturing" indefinitely.

**32. Get an I.O.U. for everything you do.**
- *What it does:* Every concession earns reciprocity.
- *How to use:* When you send info / take a meeting / extend pricing — name what you get in return.

**33. On your way to the bank, keep one eye over your shoulder.**
- *What it does:* Deals can unravel post-close; stay vigilant.
- *How to use:* Confirm commitment multiple times; watch for buyer's remorse before contract signs.

## Part Three — Course-Correct (Rules 34–49)

**34. Work smart, not hard.**
- *What it does:* Effort ≠ outcome. Pick high-leverage activities.
- *How to use:* Prioritize qualified pipeline over volume of low-fit calls.

**35. If your competition does it, stop doing it right away.**
- *What it does:* Differentiate by behavior, not just product.
- *How to use:* If every rep sends the same proposal/demo flow, invert it.

**36. Only decision makers can get others to make decisions.**
- *What it does:* Influencers can't close for you.
- *How to use:* Verify you're talking to the true economic buyer; don't let a champion "take it upstairs."

**37. All prospects lie, all the time.**
- *What it does:* Treat claims skeptically — "we're interested" rarely means it.
- *How to use:* Verify with behavior, not words; push for actions (meetings, docs, access).

**38. The problem the prospect brings you is never the real problem.**
- *What it does:* Stated pain is a symptom, not the root.
- *How to use:* Dig 3+ levels deep with "and what does that cause?" before proposing a fix.

**39. When all else fails, become a consultant.**
- *What it does:* Shift from sales posture to advisor.
- *How to use:* If the deal's stuck, drop the pitch and ask what they'd do if they were you.

**40. Fake it 'til you make it.**
- *What it does:* Behaviors precede confidence.
- *How to use:* Act like the top rep; the results train the mindset, not the reverse.

**41. There are no bad prospects — only bad salespeople.**
- *What it does:* Ownership mindset.
- *How to use:* Don't blame the lead list; fix qualification and discovery.

**42. A winner has alternatives, a loser puts all his eggs in one basket.**
- *What it does:* Pipeline health = posture.
- *How to use:* Keep enough deals that no single one matters — desperation loses deals.

**43. You don't learn how to win by getting a "yes," you learn how to win by getting a "no."**
- *What it does:* Losses teach more than wins.
- *How to use:* Do loss reviews; ask "no" prospects what actually drove their decision.

**44. When your foot hurts, you're probably standing on your own toe.**
- *What it does:* Most sales problems are self-inflicted.
- *How to use:* Audit your own behavior before blaming the prospect / product / market.

**45. Express your feelings through third-party stories.**
- *What it does:* Indirect delivery reduces defensiveness.
- *How to use:* "I had a client who thought the same — here's what happened…" instead of direct pushback.

**46. There is no such thing as a good try.**
- *What it does:* Outcomes matter, not effort.
- *How to use:* Judge activity by whether it moved the deal forward — not whether it was well-intentioned.

**47. Selling is a Broadway play performed by a psychiatrist.**
- *What it does:* You play a role (performer) while reading emotion (therapist).
- *How to use:* Stay composed and scripted outside; diagnose feelings inside.

**48. A life without risk is a life without growth.**
- *What it does:* Safety = stagnation.
- *How to use:* Take the uncomfortable ask; avoiding it has a bigger cost than making it.

**49. Leave your child in the car.**
- *What it does:* Don't let your emotional (Child ego) state drive the sales call.
- *How to use:* Show up in Adult mode — calm, analytical — regardless of how you feel.

---

*Framework foundation: Transactional Analysis (Parent / Adult / Child ego states). Sandler's core insight: "People buy emotionally and justify intellectually." Rules operationalize this by controlling seller behavior, not prospect psychology.*
