## Value Proposition 1 — The CAC Ceiling

**Pain Point addressed:** CAC has been climbing 30-40% YoY. Meta feels like it's hit a ceiling. Previous agency isn't explaining why.  
**Social Proof:** Supergut  
**Framework:** #6 — `{{Pain Point}} because {{Current Way}} fails → {{Unique Mechanism}} instead → {{Social Proof}} got {{Outcome}}`

---

**Filled template:**

| Component | Content |
|---|---|
| **Pain Point** | CAC climbing 30-40% YoY on Meta |
| **Current Way (why it fails)** | Agency is chasing ROAS targets with daily budgets — no profit model behind the spend |
| **Unique Mechanism** | Pull P&L, margins, and cohort LTV first → set exact CAC ceiling → run cost controls, not daily budgets, as the campaign structure |
| **Social Proof** | Supergut — had completely shut off Meta due to rising CPA before working with Kynship |
| **Outcome** | $100K → $1M/month in new customer revenue in 3 months |

**Value Proposition Copy:**

> CAC climbing 30-40% YoY on Meta because your agency is chasing ROAS targets with daily budgets and no profit model behind them → we pull your P&L, margins, and cohort LTV first, set your CAC ceiling, then run cost controls — not daily budgets — so Meta can only spend when customers cost what your margins allow → Supergut had completely shut off Meta before we built this system for them → $100K to $1M/month in new customer revenue in 3 months

## Value Proposition 2 — The UGC Death Loop

**Pain Point addressed:** Spending £6-9k/month on UGC retainers. Creatives fatigue in 3-5 days. The content machine is running but going nowhere.  
**Social Proof:** Black Girl Vitamins  
**Framework:** #9 — `Why {{Pain Point}}? → {{Implication}} → Unlike {{Current Way}}, we {{Unique Mechanism}} → {{Outcome}}`

---

**Filled template:**

| Component | Content |
|---|---|
| **Pain Point** | UGC retainer burning £6-9k/month; creatives dying in 3-5 days |
| **Implication** | You're buying per-asset content against a brief-to-deliver cycle longer than its shelf life — Meta's algorithm now demands 200+ archetype variants simultaneously, not 15-20 clips |
| **Current Way** | UGC platforms (Billo, Insense, Cohley) billing per deliverable with a 14-21 day brief cycle |
| **Unique Mechanism** | Seed 200-300 creators/month who generate and post through their own handles → run every variant through a cost control account structure so the algorithm finds what converts inside the CAC ceiling |
| **Social Proof** | Black Girl Vitamins |
| **Outcome** | $8M → $85M/year; seeding content drove 40% of their total ad spend for over 2 years |

---

**Value Proposition Copy:**

> Why is your UGC retainer burning £6-9k/month on creatives that die in 3-5 days? → Because you're buying per-asset content against a brief cycle longer than its shelf life — Meta now needs 200+ archetype variants simultaneously, not 15-20 clips → unlike UGC platforms billing per deliverable, we seed 200-300 creators/month and run every piece through a cost-controlled account structure so the algorithm finds what converts inside your CAC ceiling → Black Girl Vitamins scaled from $8M to $85M/year — seeding content alone drove 40% of their ad spend for over 2 years

---

**Hierarchy check (per copy_and_prompt_guidelines.md):**
- Tier 1 (40%) ✓ — Opens with the prospect's own words ("burning £6-9k/month") + surfaces the structural implication they likely don't know (brief cycle vs. creative half-life mismatch; 200+ archetype demand)
- Tier 2 (30%) ✓ — Unique mechanism is specific: 200-300 creators/month + cost controls, not "we do UGC." Social proof is same segment, same mechanism (seeding), and the number is hard to ignore
- Tier 3 (20%) ✓ — Outcome is revenue transformation ($8M → $85M), not a ROAS stat
- Tier 4 (10%) — The "40% of ad spend for 2 years" line functions as implied risk reversal (longevity of the system)

## Usage Notes

**VP 1 (CAC Ceiling)** is highest-probability for:
- Founder/CEO at $5M–$20M brand with flat YoY growth
- Director/VP of Marketing who has rotated 2+ agencies and still hitting the same wall
- Any brand that has recently complained about Meta costs publicly (LinkedIn trigger signal)

**VP 2 (UGC Death Loop)** is highest-probability for:
- Head of Growth / Performance Marketing Manager — operational pain they own directly
- Brands with visible creative fatigue (Facebook Ad Library: same 2-3 videos running 3+ months)
- UK supplement brands running a UGC retainer (VoC data specifically names this spend level)

**VP 3 (Agency That Teaches)** is highest-probability for:
- CMO at 7-8 figure brand who is over-invested in managing vendor strategy
- Founders who went in-house and fired the hire within 90 days
- Brands where the CMO/Founder is the one setting the CAC targets rather than being handed them
