When prospects say "that's different from other agencies," what SPECIFICALLY are they reacting to?
Prospects are usually reacting to how financially grounded our approach is. Most agencies report on platform metrics after the fact and then make reactive adjustments, whereas we start with the brand’s actual business model — margin profile, CAC tolerance, payback window, repeat purchase behavior, and profit goals — and use that to determine how aggressively they should scale. That is often the first moment where they realize we are not just trying to improve ad account performance, but help them make better commercial decisions.

They are also surprised by how proactive the model is. Instead of waiting for performance to drop and then reacting, we build around the inputs that drive financial outcomes in the first place — creative volume, account structure, testing velocity, and clear profit guardrails. That makes the relationship feel less like outsourced campaign management and more like having a growth partner who understands both the levers inside the ad account and the economics outside of it.

Who are your primary competitors?
Common Thread Collective is one of the clearest competitors because they also position around financial rigor, unit economics, and tying marketing performance back to broader business outcomes rather than just platform metrics. Like Kynship, they appeal to brands that want a partner who understands contribution margin, CAC tolerance, payback windows, and how to scale in a way that is commercially responsible rather than just growth-at-all-costs.

The similarity is that both companies try to elevate the conversation beyond “we run your ads” into “we help you make better growth decisions.” Both are trying to win trust by showing they understand the economics behind customer acquisition, not just the mechanics inside Meta. That makes them a relevant competitor in how they frame value to founders and operators.

Where Kynship differs is in how heavily it emphasizes the proactive inputs that actually create financial outcomes, especially creative volume, creative diversity, testing velocity, and account structure. Common Thread is strong on the strategic and financial narrative, but Kynship’s edge is that it connects those financial goals more directly to the day-to-day operating system required to hit them. In other words, Kynship is not just helping brands understand the numbers; it is helping them build the creative and paid social engine needed to change the numbers.

Another difference is in posture. Many agencies, even financially sophisticated ones, still end up acting reactively once campaigns are live. Kynship’s positioning is more proactive: set profit guardrails first, define what scale should look like commercially, then shape the creative, media buying, and testing environment around that from the outset. That makes the model feel less like reporting on performance and more like engineering performance.

The only other people in the space that go to the extent that we do would be contractors like Andrew Farris or fractional CMO's.

Why do clients choose you over COMPETITORS? Not 'we care more.' What's the SPECIFIC reason they pick you?
Clients choose Kynship because we do not just promise better ad management — we give them a clearer, more financially grounded path to scale. Most agencies optimize reactively inside the ad account after performance moves. We start higher up: understanding the brand’s margins, CAC tolerance, payback window, repeat purchase behavior, and profit goals, then building the paid social and creative system around those realities. That makes our recommendations feel more commercially sound and more trustworthy to founders who care about profitable growth, not just prettier reporting.

They also choose us because we are more proactive in how we create performance. Rather than trying to squeeze more from the same tired assets, we focus on the upstream inputs that actually move results — creative volume, creative diversity, testing velocity, and account structure. In practice, that means clients feel they are getting a system for generating better outcomes, not just a team reacting to yesterday’s numbers.

Our sales cycle also takes them fully through their own numbers. We get financials, P&L's and access to shopify / amazon - everything. We believe "the only attribution that never lies is your bank account" - we don't want to sell them a fancy dashboard to justify our retainer - we want to grow their topline and net profit at the rate we help them determine.
