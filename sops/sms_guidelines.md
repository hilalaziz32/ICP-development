# SMS Script Creation & Quality Assurance Guidelines

## Overview

This document contains the Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and Quality Assurance (QA) checklists for creating SMS copy. These guidelines ensure all text messages are properly formatted, sound human and relatable, and meet our quality standards before submission.

---

## SMS Script Creation

### Step 1: Introduction of Concept

**Three Core Questions to Address:**

1) We want to show team members all the routes they can walk
2) How to know which route to take?
3) How to take the path?

### Example: The Copywriter Framework

Let's say you're a Copywriter who specializes in Twitter.

You literally have 10-15 different types of content you can write - from client case study breakdowns, personal accomplishments, complex business problems, success story threads and many more.

When training someone on this, you want to teach them **how to know which type of content to write**.

Once you train someone on how to decide which type of content to create, you then want to train them on **how to create that specific type of content**.

**Example Continued: Case Study Thread**

Your Copywriter has decided to write a Case Study Thread. You then want to break down each part of this Case Study Thread into smaller digestible sections so it's easier for your Copywriter to learn.

In this case, you would teach them how to write a great case study hook. When writing a hook, there may be 10 different types of hooks you can write. You then want to teach them how to know when to write each different type of hook.

Once they've identified a hook to write, for each individual hook, you want to train them on how you actually write the hook.

Then you move onto the body section of the content and repeat the same process.

**Key Principle:**

As you can see, we want to showcase every single pathway that your team member can take in order to execute this task. Good training introduces how you get there and how you execute. This will create new neural networks in the brain that will help retain this information.

## SMS Quality Assurance (QA) Procedure

### Objective

This SOP outlines the steps for conducting a quality assurance check on SMS messages to ensure they meet the required standards before submission.

### Who & When?

- **Outbound Manager** before submitting copies to AT & ticking it off their P.M + troubleshooting
- **Client Success Manager** as she conducts QA reviews

### Reference Video

Watch the QA procedure walkthrough: https://www.loom.com/share/44767338f9b542128c6c12f5edcc0c6f

## Step-by-Step QA Guide

### 1. Initiate QA Process

- Begin by reviewing the SMS script creation process
- Ensure that you have the QA checklist ready to use

### 2. Complete the QA Checklist

**CRITICAL RULE:** Do not submit the SMS unless all items on the QA checklist are ticked off.

### 3. Revise SMS Based on Feedback

- Make necessary changes to ensure the SMS **sounds more human and relatable**
- Ensure the message **explains why you are reaching out**

---

## SMS QA Checklist

Before submitting any SMS copy, verify that ALL of the following criteria are met:

### ✅ Human & Conversational
- [ ] **Sounds human & casual?** (not corporate)
- [ ] **Does it sound like 1-to-1 text?** (not mass blast)
- [ ] **Read it out loud** → Does it sound smooth/free flowing or congested/nerdy?

### ✅ Clear & Specific
- [ ] **Specific outcome clearly stated?** (e.g., "6-7 leads", not "more leads")
- [ ] **Explains why you are reaching out** to this specific person

### ✅ Low Friction
- [ ] **Frictionless CTA?** (Call-to-action is easy and non-committal)

### ✅ Response-Worthy
- [ ] **Would YOU respond to this?** (Put yourself in the recipient's shoes)

## Critical Failure Pattern: Generic Pain Without Unique Mechanism

### The Unique Mechanism (UM) Requirement

**Example Feedback from QA:**

> "Target Market (Flooring) = Mid/High Soph, Problem Aware. This SMS fails because it uses **generic pain without a Unique Mechanism (UM)**. Needs a **specific mechanism + micro proof**."

### What This Means

An SMS **fails QA** if it:
- Uses generic pain points without explaining HOW you solve it differently
- Lacks a Unique Mechanism (UM) - your specific methodology or approach
- Doesn't include micro proof (specific evidence or results)

### What You Need Instead

✅ **Specific mechanism** - Explain your unique approach or methodology  
✅ **Micro proof** - Include concrete evidence (numbers, timeframes, specific results)

### Example of Failure vs. Success

**❌ FAILS - Generic Pain:**
> "Struggling with lead generation? We can help you get more leads."

**✅ PASSES - Specific Mechanism + Micro Proof:**
> "We don't just run ads - we cut 22% in ad spend within 45 days for Stripe by embedding anomaly detection directly into their pipeline, which led to $1.1M in budget savings."

---

## Key Principles Summary

1. **No submission without full checklist completion** - All items must be ticked off
2. **Sounds human and relatable** - Must pass the "read aloud" test
3. **Explains the "why"** - Why this person, why now
4. **Unique Mechanism required** - Never use generic pain without a specific mechanism
5. **Include micro proof** - Back up claims with specific evidence
