# Copy and Prompt Guidelines

## Prompting Rules
**The 80/20 Rule for Prompt Engineering**
Don't overcomplicate your prompts. Focus on the 20% of effort that gets 80% of the results by using this simple template:
- **Context** - Overview of what you're doing
- **Objective** - Specific instructions
- **Inputs** - What data you're providing
- **Guidelines** - Constraints like "limit to 3 words" or "add xyz"
- **Examples** - 5-10 examples whenever possible (biggest game changer)

This structure will handle most use cases without spending hours perfecting prompts.

## The Hierarchy of Value Proposition Creation
**The Pyramid of Persuasion (In Order of Importance)**

### 🥇 Tier 1: Specific Problem + Implication (40% of impact)
Why it's #1: If you don't identify a real, painful problem AND its consequences, nothing else matters.
- **A. The Specific Problem:** Observable and verifiable, happening right now, measurable impact, not generic fluff.
- **B. The Implication/Consequence:** What happens if they DON'T solve this, the compound effect over time.
*(Example: "CAC tripled → Burning $50k/mo = 8 months less runway")*

### 🥈 Tier 2: Trust Elements (30% of impact)
- **A. Unique Mechanism:** They've likely tried solutions before. They need to know why yours is different (Different approach than what failed, Logical reason why it works, Simple to understand, Not just a fancy name).
- **B. Social Proof:** In a world of big claims, proof is everything (Same industry + same problem + specific result, Similar problem + specific result, Any quantified result, Recognizable client names).

### 🥉 Tier 3: Outcome (20% of impact)
Why it's not #1 anymore: Everyone promises outcomes. "Get more leads!" "10x your revenue!" It's noise. Rather, they need to see exactly what success looks like for THEM (specificity).
- **Chunking Up:** Company valuation → Revenue → Profit
- **Chunking Down:** Revenue → Customers → Leads → Clicks

### 🏅 Tier 4: Risk Reversal (10% of impact)
Why it's last: Leading with guarantees screams desperation in 2025. It's expected, not exceptional.
- Better approach: Weave it naturally: "...which is why we only charge after delivering the first 50 qualified leads"
- Supports, doesn't lead.
- Expected baseline.
