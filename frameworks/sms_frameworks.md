## SMS Script Creation: Core Concept

### Introduction to the Framework Approach

When creating SMS copy, we want to show team members **all the routes they can walk**, teach them **how to know which route to take**, and train them on **how to take that path**.

#### The Pathway Method

**Example: The Copywriter Framework**

Let's say you're a Copywriter who specializes in Twitter. You literally have 10-15 different types of content you can write - from client case study breakdowns, personal accomplishments, complex business problems, success story threads and many more.

When training someone on this, you want to teach them **how to know which type of content to write**.

Once you train someone on how to decide which type of content to create, you then want to train them on **how to create that specific type of content**.

**Example Continued: Case Study Thread**

Your Copywriter has decided to write a Case Study Thread. You then want to break down each part of this Case Study Thread into smaller digestible sections so it's easier for your Copywriter to learn.

In this case, you would teach them how to write a great case study hook. When writing a hook, there may be 10 different types of hooks you can write. You then want to teach them how to know when to write each different type of hook.

Once they've identified a hook to write, for each individual hook, you want to train them on how you actually write the hook.

Then you move onto the body section of the content and repeat the same process.

**Key Principle:**

We want to showcase every single pathway that your team member can take in order to execute this task. Good training introduces how you get there and how you execute. This will create new neural networks in the brain that will help retain this information.

## SMS Framework Formula: Pain + Mechanism + Outcome

All high-performing SMS messages follow this core structure:

**Pain Point + Implication → Unique Mechanism → Social Proof + Outcome**

This formula ensures your SMS:
- Addresses a specific problem the prospect faces
- Explains HOW you solve it differently (mechanism)
- Proves credibility with real results (social proof + outcome)

## Key SMS Copywriting Principles

1. **Be Specific:** Use exact numbers ("8-11 qualified appointments" not "more appointments")

2. **Show the Mechanism:** Always explain HOW you do it differently (e.g., "trigger-based SMS to companies hiring CMOs" vs. "we do SMS outreach")

3. **Include Micro Proof:** Name-drop real companies and specific results

4. **Address the Pain First:** Start with what they're experiencing now

5. **Contrast with Old Way:** Show why their current approach isn't working

6. **Keep It Conversational:** SMS should sound like a 1-to-1 text, not a mass blast

7. **Create Urgency Through Triggers:** Reference hiring signals, time constraints, or current events

8. **Multiple Pathways to Same Destination:** Different frameworks achieve the same goal - choose based on:
   - Market sophistication level
   - Prospect awareness stage
   - Available social proof
   - Strength of unique mechanism

## When to Use Which Framework

**Use Framework 1-3 when:** You have strong proof and need to establish credibility quickly

**Use Framework 4-6 when:** Targeting a specific ICP with known pain points

**Use Framework 7-9 when:** Addressing complex, multi-faceted problems or skeptical prospects

**Use Framework 10-12 when:** You have a compelling case study story or need to reframe their thinking
