    <FailedAttempt id="FA-01" mapsToPain="IP-01">
      <WhatTheyTried>Hiring a single internal designer to keep the ad library full.</WhatTheyTried>
      <WhyItFailed>Structural undersizing. One designer cannot produce the 200–300 monthly variants Andromeda-era Meta requires; the briefing-to-launch cycle becomes the bottleneck no matter how skilled the designer is. The role collapses into "campaign delivery manager" and creative output stalls.</WhyItFailed>
      <VerbatimQuote source="call18_Jupiter_Laura_GM" url="internal://Skill2/IP-01">"I have one internal designer... and she has not been able to keep up with the requests."</VerbatimQuote>
      <ImplicationForOutbound>Acknowledge the in-house designer as the right instinct (control, brand voice) — then frame the volume gap as a structural math problem, not a hiring problem. Position Kynship's seeding + UGC database + AI static stack as the layer that finally lets the in-house designer be a creative director instead of a production line.</ImplicationForOutbound>
    </FailedAttempt>

    <FailedAttempt id="FA-02" mapsToPain="IP-01">
      <WhatTheyTried>Stacking a UGC agency retainer ($6K–$9K/month) on top of an in-house designer.</WhatTheyTried>
      <WhyItFailed>Time-to-value collapse. Winning UGC ads now fatigue in 3–5 days, but the brief→shoot→deliver cycle on a retainer is 14–21 days. The brand pays for content that arrives already behind the algorithm.</WhyItFailed>
      <VerbatimQuote source="VoC_DTC_Growth_Insights_source2" url="internal://Skill2/IP-01">"we spend another six to nine thousand pounds a month on UGC content... if the ad only works for three days or five days... So we're constantly feeding the beast."</VerbatimQuote>
      <ImplicationForOutbound>Don't trash the UGC agency category — name the velocity gap. Lead with: "Most UGC retainers were priced for a 14-day creative half-life. Yours is now 5." Then introduce seeding-driven volume that eliminates the cycle entirely.</ImplicationForOutbound>
    </FailedAttempt>

    <FailedAttempt id="FA-03" mapsToPain="IP-02 IP-04">
      <WhatTheyTried>Migrating 90% of Meta budget to AppLovin to escape rising CAC.</WhatTheyTried>
      <WhyItFailed>Channel substitution without unit-economics fix. AppLovin doesn't surface frequency, so the brand burns audiences at unknown saturation. Same architectural problem (no P&L-anchored cost ceiling) on a new surface.</WhyItFailed>

    </FailedAttempt>

  <Blockers>

    <Blocker id="BL-01" type="budget">
      <Description>Sticker shock at $15K–$25K/mo retainers — not because the cash isn't there but because every prior agency at that price point under-delivered, so the line item now reads "risk" rather than "investment."</Description>
      <Evidence source="call18_Jupiter_Laura_GM" url="internal://Skill2/PP-04">"That's quite a bit more than what I'm paying today... I just need to figure out how to fit that into my budget."</Evidence>
      <ImplicationForOutbound>Lead with the free 8–10 hour deep audit deliverable in cold copy, not the retainer. Move the first commitment from "sign a contract" to "review my P&L model" — that is a 10x lower psychological cost and converts the retainer conversation from leap-of-faith to numbers-on-paper.</ImplicationForOutbound>
    </Blocker>

    <Blocker id="BL-02" type="tech">
      <Description>Attribution stack fragmentation — Shopify, Meta Ads Manager, Triple Whale/Northbeam, and post-purchase surveys all report different numbers. Founders cannot make a confident reallocation decision when no single source of truth exists.</Description>
      <Evidence source="VoC_DTC_Growth_Insights_source42" url="internal://Skill2/IP-05">"If you're going to do an attribution of like, this was a Facebook traffic or a Google traffic, I do not trust Shopify to do that attribution."</Evidence>
      <ImplicationForOutbound>Skip the attribution debate entirely. Anchor the conversation on bank-account revenue ÷ new customers (blended AMER) and frame Kynship as the agency that decided to stop arguing about pixels.</ImplicationForOutbound>
    </Blocker>

    <Blocker id="BL-03" type="knowledge">
      <Description>Founders and CMOs lack the technical expertise to evaluate whether an agency or in-house hire is structurally competent — so selection happens on rapport, case studies, and price, not on diagnostic rigor. This guarantees the cycle of failed engagements continues.</Description>
      <Evidence source="call21_WOWSports_Jason" url="internal://Skill2/IP-07">"it's difficult for us to really manage this without a whole lot of internal Knowledge ourselves."</Evidence>
      <ImplicationForOutbound>Outbound copy should include one diagnostic question the prospect cannot easily answer (e.g., "What is your CAC payback window net of returns and discounts?"). The unanswerable question reframes Kynship as the people who think one layer deeper than the prospect's current partner.</ImplicationForOutbound>
    </Blocker>

    <Blocker id="BL-04" type="bandwidth">
      <Description>The CMO and founder are already absorbing the agency-management tax — 3–5 hours/week writing briefs, fact-checking reports, and managing the relationship. They have no calendar slack to evaluate, onboard, or transition to a new partner even when they know the current one is wrong.</Description>
      <Evidence source="call4_Drapeezi_Racheal_CMO" url="internal://Skill2/IP-03">"I am teaching them a little bit more than they're actually teaching us."</Evidence>
      <ImplicationForOutbound>Promise an asynchronous, founder-light onboarding. Specifically commit to "less than 2 hours of your time in the first 30 days" — this directly neutralises the bandwidth objection that kills most agency switches even when intent is high.</ImplicationForOutbound>
    </Blocker>

    <Blocker id="BL-05" type="political">
      <Description>Switching agencies mid-year forces the CMO or Head of Paid Media to publicly admit that their last hire decision was wrong. For senior leaders whose credibility is tied to that decision, the political cost of switching can exceed the operational cost of staying.</Description>
      <Evidence source="call4_Drapeezi_Racheal_CMO" url="internal://Skill2/IP-03">"The general feedback was it didn't work because we couldn't scale."</Evidence>
      <ImplicationForOutbound>Frame the transition as an addition, not a replacement: a free deep audit + financial forecast that the CMO can present to leadership as their own initiative. Give the senior buyer a way to own the win internally before any switching cost is paid.</ImplicationForOutbound>
    </Blocker>

  <GapAnalysis>
    <UniqueMechanismOpportunity>
      Every category in the current landscape solves one slice and leaves the other three open: Foreplay/Motion fix workflow but not volume; UGC marketplaces fix volume but not testing structure; Triple Whale/Northbeam fix visibility but not the CAC ceiling; performance agencies fix execution but not strategy. Kynship's unique mechanism is the vertical stack across all four — a 12-month financial forecast (strategy), engineered cost controls anchored to the P&L (CAC ceiling), 200–300 monthly assets via seeding + UGC database + AI static (volume), and a 60/40 testing framework (decision system) — delivered through a senior two-person pod that costs less than a single Head of Growth hire. The mechanism slots precisely into the gap left by the failed attempts: it is the agency that the CMO does not have to teach, that the founder does not have to manage, and whose first deliverable is a P&L model rather than a media plan.
    </UniqueMechanismOpportunity>
