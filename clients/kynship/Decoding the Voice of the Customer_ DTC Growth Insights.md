Here is the Market Insights Report detailing the Voice of Customer (VoC) insights extracted from your 1st-party data.

### 1\. Verbatim Pain Points & Implications

**Pain Point 1: Severe Creative Bottlenecks & High Content Churn**

* **The Pain:** Brands are unable to produce a high enough volume of quality ad creatives to keep up with ad fatigue. Internal designers are overwhelmed, and external agencies are either too slow or producing off-brand, ineffective content.  
* **The Implication:** This results in wasted ad spend on rapidly fatiguing ads, stalled growth, and highly expensive in-house production costs that eat into CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) margins.  
* **Verbatim Quotes:**  
* "I have one internal designer... and she has not been able to keep up with the requests." 1  
* "We spend another six to nine thousand pounds a month on UGC content... and if the ad only works for three days or five days, it's such a high turnover and ads they start to not perform after a very short period of time. So we're constantly feeding the beast." 2  
* "I definitely am like in the camp of work smarter, not harder. And I feel like currently the ad library is working not smart and not hard." 3

**Pain Point 2: Agencies Lacking Agility, P\&L Integration, and True Partnership**

* **The Pain:** Prospects are frustrated with legacy media buying agencies that operate in silos, rely on daily budgets rather than profit targets, and act reactively rather than proactively.  
* **The Implication:** Founders and CMOs are forced to micromanage their agencies, teaching them the business instead of receiving strategic guidance, which wastes executive time and limits enterprise value growth.  
* **Verbatim Quotes:**  
* "I am teaching them a little bit more than they're actually teaching us." 4  
* "I'm sick of this like performance agency kind of in a condescending way telling me that like I don't get performance ads. Like we need to be running ugly ads." 5  
* "What we're finding is just the focus isn't there and the ability to just be agile and adjust." 6

**Pain Point 3: Unprofitable Scaling and Rising CAC on Meta**

* **The Pain:** Brands are experiencing climbing acquisition costs on major platforms like Meta, making it extremely difficult to remain profitable on first orders.  
* **The Implication:** They are pulling back spend, pausing campaigns, or drastically lowering their contribution margins, effectively capping their own revenue potential.  
* **Verbatim Quotes:**  
* "Our CAC is just going up. It went up I'm thinking 10% this over the last year and it's already high, too high... with a 30 pound CAC we're obviously running too hot there." 2  
* "We struggled to get Meta to be profitable at scale and we look, we want it to be, we're desperate to get there and we just found the cost of converting someone on Meta being too expensive." 7  
* "Our ROAS is fairly low right now, about 0.6... what we need to do is get to... at least 1 1/2 to 2 roas there to find some opportunity to be kind of break even." 8

### 2\. Current Solutions & Blockers

**Current Solutions Being Used:**

* **Agencies & Partners:** They are employing traditional media buying and creative agencies (e.g., Homestead 9, CTC 10, Raindrop 11, Block and TAM 12, Advisory 13).  
* **In-House Shifts:** Firing agencies to bring media buying and content creation back in-house to maintain closer control over the brand and margin 9, 14\.  
* **Channel Diversification:** Migrating ad spend away from Meta to platforms like AppLovin 7, TikTok Ads 15, and Google PMax 16 to chase cheaper traffic.  
* **Manual Budget Pacing:** Capping daily ad budgets based on fixed monthly allocations rather than performance/demand 17, 18\.

**Why These Solutions Are Failing:**

* In-house teams lack cross-channel insight (like understanding the "halo effect" between Meta ads and Amazon sales) and lack the bandwidth to generate hundreds of creative iterations 2, 19\.  
* Current agencies are not tying their media buying directly to the company's financial metrics (P\&L/Contribution Margins), acting as order-takers rather than strategic growth partners 20\.

**Current Blockers:**

* **Bandwidth & Technical Knowledge:** "Roz and I just don't know what we don't know... that world is changing very dynamically." 21  
* **Supply Chain / Inventory Limits:** Scaling ad spend is blocked because they run out of physical stock when demand spikes 22\.  
* **Budget Restraints:** High minimum monthly retainers ($15k-$25k) are steep for bootstrapped startups or brands currently paying budget agencies $8k-$9k/month 23, 24\.

### 3\. Dream Outcomes & Buying Triggers

**Dream Outcomes & Metrics:**

* **P\&L-Driven Media Buying:** They want an agency that ties directly to their Shopify/Amazon bank accounts and acts like a "fractional head of growth" 25, 26\.  
* **Hitting Aggressive Revenue Milestones:** E.g., taking a brand from $10M to $20M 27, reaching a £100M run rate in 3-4 years 28, or scaling D2C specifically to an $8M run rate to match retail growth 29\.  
* **High-Volume Creative Pipeline:** A seamless flow of UGC (User Generated Content), IGC (Influencer Generated Content), CGC (Customer Generated Content), and AI statics to continuously test new audiences 30, 31\.

**Buying Triggers:**

* **Failed Hires/Agencies:** The recent firing of an internal growth hire or letting go of an underperforming agency right before a critical season 4, 32\.  
* **M\&A / Leadership Transitions:** Buying a distressed business and needing to separate the teams, or appointing a new General Manager/CMO who needs a trusted vendor to hit ambitious new goals 33, 34\.  
* **Podcast/Peer Recommendations:** Hearing success stories (specifically the Purdy & Figg case study) on industry podcasts or private founder networks 35-37.

### 4\. Common Objections & Limiting Beliefs

**Objection 1: Retainer Price & Affordability**

* **The Belief:** $15k-$25k/month is too expensive, especially when current agencies cost half that, or when the brand is bootstrapping.  
* **Verbatim Quotes:**  
* "That's quite a bit more than what I'm paying today... I just need to figure out how to fit that into my budget." 38  
* "I mean it wouldn't be viable for me at the minute but I do think I need all of this." 39

**Objection 2: Fear of Cost Controls Limiting Scale**

* **The Belief:** Using "Cost Caps" or "Cost Controls" will choke off ad volume, limiting the ability to spend aggressively and grow top-line revenue.  
* **Verbatim Quotes:**  
* "The general feedback was it didn't work because we couldn't scale." 20  
* "I think it just wasn't from like their approach right with like cost caps was like you know, throw everything at the wall... The system just wasn't working for us as a business." 40, 41

**Objection 3: Mistrust of "Blended" Metrics & Attribution**

* **The Belief:** Looking strictly at the overall P\&L or bank account (Blended CAC/AMER) obscures channel-specific performance, making it hard to trust where the money is actually going.  
* **Verbatim Quotes:**  
* "If you're going to do an attribution of like, this was a Facebook traffic or a Google traffic, I do not trust Shopify to do that attribution." 42  
* "It sounds like you're just rolling in all organic and direct traffic and immediately that will obviously if we did that, we get blended roas... and we immediately drop the cost of our CAC because we're blending it in." 43

### 5\. Industry Lingo & Terminology

Term/Phrase,Context/Meaning  
AMER / NC ROAS,"Acquisition Media Efficiency Ratio / New Customer Return on Ad Spend. A blended metric looking at total revenue from new customers divided by total ad spend 44, 45."  
Cost Controls / Cost Caps,"A bid strategy on Meta/Google where the algorithm is strictly told not to exceed a target Cost Per Acquisition (CPA), prioritizing efficiency over spending the daily budget 46, 47."  
UGC / IGC / CGC,"User-Generated Content, Influencer-Generated Content, and Customer-Generated Content. Different pipelines of human-forward video assets used for ad creatives 31."  
LTV to CAC,"Lifetime Value to Customer Acquisition Cost. The ratio comparing how much a customer is worth long-term against how much it cost to acquire them 48, 49."  
Halo Effect,"The indirect sales lift on Amazon or in physical retail stores resulting from brand awareness driven by direct-to-consumer (DTC) Meta or Google ads 50, 51."  
Whitelisting / Spark Ads,"Running paid advertisements through an influencer or creator’s actual social media handle rather than the brand’s handle to increase authenticity 37, 52."  
Contribution Margin (CM),"The selling price per unit minus the variable costs (COGS, shipping, fulfillment, ad spend). Used to determine true profitability 53, 54."  
Top of Funnel (TOF),"Awareness-stage marketing aimed at new audiences who are not yet familiar with the brand 55, 56."  
Daily Budget / Highest Volume,"Traditional media buying structure where a set daily budget is given to the platform to spend entirely, regardless of day-to-day performance fluctuations 57, 58."  
COGS,"Cost of Goods Sold. The direct costs attributable to the production of the products sold 59, 60."  
Seeding,"Sending free products to micro-influencers with no contractual obligations, hoping they post organically so the brand can repurpose the content for ads 31, 61."  
BFCM,"Black Friday / Cyber Monday. The most critical, high-volume Q4 sales period for D2C e-commerce brands 62, 63."
