# Process: GTM Data Sheet Completion for Outbound Managers

**Objective:** Convert onboarding insights + case studies into a GTM Data Sheet with clear, scalable segments, suitable for Apollo/Clay/sales ops execution.

**Step 1: Review the Onboarding Form (15–20 mins)**
Extract the core inputs that define how the client wins.
1. Read the client’s onboarding form in full.
2. Note:
   - Core services offered (e.g., SEO, product marketing, lead gen)
   - Typical buyer personas / industries they serve
   - Clues about target company size and sales cycle
   - Client commentary on customer pain points or messaging struggles
3. Use this to inform audience definition and offer positioning.

**Step 2: Analyze Client Case Studies (15–20 mins)**
Use real success stories to define your “like-for-like” segments.
1. Identify 2–4 relevant case studies from the client.
2. For each case, record:
   - What type of company it was (industry, size, tech complexity)
   - Who the key buyers were (job titles)
   - What offer or service was delivered
   - The measurable result (e.g., leads, revenue, funding)
3. Match these wins to repeatable segments and add to a shortlist.

**Step 3: Create ICP Segments (20 mins)**
Define 3–5 segments that combine case study wins and ICP logic.
1. Use industry + size + intent to define each segment:
2. Examples:
   - “Retail & Commerce Tech Platforms” (based on Fabric)
   - “Integration/API SaaS Companies” (based on DigiBee)
   - “HR Tech Vendors doing lead gen” (based on HiQ-Matic)
3. Ensure each segment is:
   - Clear enough for data teams to pull in Apollo or Clay
   - Scalable (not niche down too far unless strategic)
   - Supported by a real-world client win or rationale

**Step 4: Add Trigger Signals (10–15 mins)**
Define why now triggers for each segment to justify timing of outreach.
1. For each segment, include 2–3 of the following where relevant:
   - Hiring for product marketers or GTM roles
   - No marketing department visible on LinkedIn
   - Recently raised funding (Seed to Series C)
   - Using complex language/tech on their website
   - Active in technical industries (API, AI, cyber, fintech, etc.)
   - SEO-heavy traffic or ad spend without clear messaging

**Step 5: Fill the GTM Data Sheet (20–30 mins)**
Translate your strategy into a structured and repeatable format.
For each segment, complete the following fields:

| Field | How to Fill It |
|---|---|
| **Segment/Niche** | Use specific, clear terms (e.g., “Retail Tech Platforms”) |
| **Job Titles** | Buyer roles based on case studies or intuition (e.g., CTO, VP Product) |
| **Company Size** | Use case study or intuition (e.g., “50–500 employees”) |
| **Notes** | Add filters or constraints (e.g., “No marketing head on LinkedIn”) |
| **TAM Estimate** | Estimate based on Apollo search (optional, if known) |
| **Offer / Angle** | Reference case study or write messaging angle (e.g., “Simplify complex tech messaging”) |
| **Signal 1–3** | Add 2–3 trigger points (e.g., “Hiring PMM”, “Recently funded”, “No CMO listed”) |

**Step 6: Final Review & Handoff (5–10 mins)**
QA for clarity, relevance, and execution-readiness.
1. Make sure:
   - Segments are descriptive enough for the data team
   - Job titles are recognizable and scalable in sales tools
   - Offers are logically linked to service capability or past wins
2. Submit to shared workspace or GTM board.
3. Notify relevant team (data ops, copywriters, campaign manager).
